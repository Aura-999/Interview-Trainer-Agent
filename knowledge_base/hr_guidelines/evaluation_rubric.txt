INTERVIEW EVALUATION RUBRIC — COMPETENCY FRAMEWORK
====================================================

SCORING SCALE
1 — No evidence / below expectations
2 — Partial evidence / developing
3 — Meets expectations / competent
4 — Exceeds expectations / strong
5 — Exceptional / role model

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
TECHNICAL COMPETENCIES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Problem Solving
  5: Breaks down ambiguous problems methodically; identifies edge cases proactively; optimises beyond first solution.
  3: Identifies correct approach; solves the main problem; misses some edge cases.
  1: Unable to form a structured approach; guesses.

Code Quality
  5: Clean, readable, well-named; considers maintainability and testability by default.
  3: Working code; some readability issues; tests mentioned but not always implemented.
  1: Working code only; no consideration of quality.

System Design
  5: Identifies scale requirements; decomposes into services; discusses trade-offs of multiple options.
  3: Proposes a reasonable architecture; limited trade-off analysis.
  1: High-level only; no component breakdown.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BEHAVIOURAL COMPETENCIES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

STAR Response Quality
  5: Specific, concise STAR with quantified Result; directly answers the competency probed.
  3: Complete STAR structure; result present but not quantified; minor tangents.
  1: Vague; missing Task or Result; mostly hypothetical ("I would...").

Communication
  5: Clear, structured; adapts depth to audience; listens actively; no jargon without explanation.
  3: Generally clear; occasional unclear explanations; answers the question asked.
  1: Disorganised; difficult to follow; fails to address the question.

Collaboration & Teamwork
  5: Specific examples of elevating teammates; navigates conflict constructively; credits others.
  3: Works well in teams; one solid example; limited evidence of proactive collaboration.
  1: Vague team references; positions self as sole contributor.

Growth Mindset
  5: Proactively seeks feedback; clear examples of adapting after failure; learns across domains.
  3: Accepts feedback; learns from stated mistakes; limited proactive examples.
  1: Defensive about failures; no evidence of learning from feedback.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
RED FLAGS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
- Answers entirely hypothetical ("I would...") rather than specific ("I did...")
- Unable to recall concrete examples under probing
- Blames teammates or manager without self-reflection
- Cannot explain technical choices beyond surface level
- Dismisses alternative approaches without reasoning
